Animal Well and the fun of stopping to play
So the other day I played animal well, around 20 hours of it.
It is a really well crafted game, its controls are good and tight enabling for some snappy platforming and fast movement, which come in handy with how large the map is.
The visuals are gorgeous, the spriteart is simple but is elevated by the layering of shaders that make the game look like its played on a CRT. It works well with the spriteart to create a sort of modern retro I have not seen in quite a bit.
The puzzles in it, at least on the base layer are quite neat, you have to think about some and others are pretty obvious
Now Spoilers: I did not play much past the credits and did none of the ARG puzzles and stuff, as I think that moment has passed and frankly you cant really engage with the game online without just getting handed the results to some puzzles instantly.
That compounding with my frustrations with some of the game stopped me from engaging with it further after the upgraded bubble wand.
Now onto real hater hours: The game is about 30% - 40% too large.
By the time I came to the last flame (the dog which holy fuck that ones pretty bad) I was genuienly over the whole thing, the many eggs were fun (I did not get them all, I was maybe missing 10 or so) there could have been 20% less of them and it would have been the same.
The puzzles that opened up more puzzles were really fun and novel around the first half of the game, but as it progresed and the game opend up wider and wider and when a end came in sight the more complex ones frankly felt like they needed a degree in cryptology to complete. Combine that with the general lack of (to me) satisfying rewards and I felt like I was genuinely working towards nothing.
The backtracking was also really agressive, and even though you get a fast travel hub and a warp to it, I found myslef spending more time getting places than actually getting things done, I love sinking my teeth into a fair jumping puzzle for example but traversing most of the map felt pretty trivial.
Id say that it would lend itself to the exploration, but by the time you are getting the tools for the more advanced puzzles and movemen you are just going through the motions to pick up clues to backtrack to another location to pick up more clues.
Now part of that I blame on the fact that through the whole playthrough I never felt rewarded finding any of the eggs or solving any of the puzzles and thats genuinely on me I think, adhd and the attentionspan of a fly really didnt set me up for that. I should have stopped playing about 10 hours in but I promised I'd give it a fair try and I think I did.
However, I think unless you dont really love these sort of escalating puzzles, its not for you. In the end I think the strength of the game were misplaced, for how well it controlled it should have leaned a bit more into the bosses, each of them felt like a afterhtought, being either too easy or poorly designed.
That culminated for me at the Dog, which stated previously, was pretty bad. I got its flame last and it was by far the most tedious of them all, Usually I am up for a challenge, I did a few playthroughs of AC6, beat that before they nerfed the bosses and did the Shadow Crytal form the Knight in Deltarune.
Even though I chose to skip forward with the whistle, it still felt insanely unfair. It rubberbanded super hard, did super small circles and in key rooms you kinda have to go through, summoned more dogs that were just cluttering the screen.
Combine that with the fact that it knocks you back when you get hit and you have a problem of trying to dodge three horizontaly fast moving danger areas while one of them homes into you like a stinger missile.
The final boss of the game was a cakewalk compared to tha dawg
I think I will watch someone play the rest of the game to get a idea for the last few puzzles and maybe a speedrun to see what that looks like, but for me this was a pretty miserable experience

So overall I think its around a 6/10 with a Star for me, I would reccomend it if you like to sink your teeth into puzzles, love escape rooms and the sort. I would not reccomend it if you like metroidvanias and platformers, for that there is enough there but you should not bother with the optional stuff.
