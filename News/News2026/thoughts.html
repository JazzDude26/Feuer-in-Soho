Lupin III and the development of a comic
As I said in my previous blog post, I have been on a Lupin III Manga bender recently, and gotten to the point in the series where the main cast is introduced fully. Meaning, Lupin, Jigen, Fujiko and Goemon
And I have to say, once monkey punch figured out the direction of the manga it relly picked up. This was around chapter 17, somewhere there. Before it felt very disjointed.
And the Manga said it was but it was really extreme, the only constant till this point were basically Lupin and Zenigata, exposition happened to be delivered by random people, now it is often Jigen who does so.
Additionally it felt like Monkey Punch was not yet used tomaking the manga, which I think stems from the fact that Lupin was supposed to only run for 3 month but got popular.
So I guess it makes sense that by the point where it got more support and got to run basically indefinetly, Monkey Puch was more comfortable to establish more characters instead of one-offs.
Also it took till that chapter for him to not have every female charater that shows up be assaulted :/ idk it was the 60s
Either way I am glad it picked up in quality in that sense because it really started to drag, I have yet to see major improvements in his art, but the main casts are getting more consistent.
Also I really like that he uses Jigen for exposition now, sometimes Jigen even breaks the 4th wall just to say "good I showed up to deliver exposition lol" its kinda cute.
But yea he settled in a rythm now I think and has nailed the characters down, wich gives the manga, even though its still a "heist of the week" type deal a more consistent throughline, kinda like Star Trek or Scooby Doo
