Animal Well and stopping to be fun
So the other day I played animal well, around 20 hours of it. It is a really beatiful game that controlls exellently with fun puzzles.
My problem with it is that it is about 40% too long for what it is.
By the time I came to the last flame (the dog which holy fuck that ones pretty bad) I was genuienly over the whole thing, the many eggs were fun (I did not get them all) there could have been 20% less of them and it would have been the same.
The puzzles that opened up more puzzles were really fun and novel around the first half of the game, but as it progresed and the game opend up wider and wider without a end in sight, I felt like I was genuinely working towards nothing.
The backtracking was also really agressive, and even though you get a fast travel hub and a warp to it, I found myslef spending more time getting places than actually getting things done. Id say that it would lend itself to the exploration, but by the time you are getting the tools for the more advanced puzzles and movemen you are just going through the motions to pick up clues to backtrack to another location to pik up more clues.
Now part of that I blame on the fact that through the whole playthrough I never felt rewarded finding any of the eggs or solving any of the puzzles and thats genuinely on me I think, adhd and the attentionspan of a fly really didnt set me up for that. And thats on me, I should have stopped playing about 10 hours in but I promised id give it a fair try and I think I did.
However I think unless you dont really love these sort of escalating puzzles, its not for you. In the end I think the strength of the game were misplaced, for how well it controlled it should have leaned a bit more into the bosses, each of them felt like a afterhtought, being either too easy or poorly designed.
That culminated for me at the Dog, which stated previously, was pretty bad. I got its flame last and it was by far the most tedious of them all, Usually I am up for a challenge, I did a few playthroughs of AC6, beat that before the nerf and did the Shadow Crytal form the Knight in Deltarune.
Even though I chose to skip forward with the whistle, it still felt insanely unfair. It rubberbanded super hard, did super small circles and in key rooms summoned more dogs that were just cluttering the dog. Combine that with the fact that it knocks you back when you get hit and you have a problem.

So overall I think its around a 5/10 for me, I would reccomend it if you like to sink your teeth into puzzles, love escape rooms and the sort. I would not reccomend it if you like metroidvanias and platformers, for that there is enough there but you should not bother with the optional stuff.
